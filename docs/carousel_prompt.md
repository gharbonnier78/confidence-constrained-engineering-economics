# 10-slide carousel prompt

Create a premium 10-slide LinkedIn carousel based on the article **Confidence-Constrained Engineering Economics**.

Style: clean executive consulting style, white/light background, deep navy/graphite text, electric blue accents, amber for risk, minimal icons, strong diagrams, readable on mobile.

Tone: systems engineering, FinOps, architecture governance, executive decision-making.

Do not mention STRAT-Q. This is a foundation article.

## Slide 1 - Hook

**FinOps is becoming the new trade-off map of engineering**

Not just cloud cost optimization. A new way to arbitrate cost, risk, sovereignty, security, delivery and AI dependency.

Visual: engineering decision map with arrows between cost, risk, security, sovereignty, AI compute.

## Slide 2 - The old question is obsolete

The old question: **What is the cheapest architecture?**

The new question: **What is the minimum cost that buys defensible confidence?**

Visual: two-column contrast, cost minimization vs confidence-constrained decision.

## Slide 3 - Engineering choices are now coupled

SaaS vs on-premise. Cloud vs sovereign cloud. Staging vs pre-production. Canary vs blue/green. External AI API vs internal inference.

None of these decisions are independent.

Visual: network graph of coupled trade-offs.

## Slide 4 - SaaS is not just SaaS

SaaS can accelerate delivery, but it can also introduce supplier dependency, data exposure, jurisdictional risk and exit-cost risk.

On-premise can increase control, but only if the organization has the security and operational maturity to run it.

Visual: balanced scale, SaaS on one side, on-premise on the other.

## Slide 5 - Staging is not a server

A representative environment is a risk-compression asset. It buys evidence, reduces residual risk, prevents teams from blocking each other, but its cost can grow super-linearly.

Visual: staging environment as bridge between design and production.

## Slide 6 - Deployment patterns are risk controls

Blue/green buys reversibility. Canary reduces blast radius. Feature flags decouple deployment from exposure. Shadow traffic buys evidence before impact. Parallel run buys institutional trust.

Visual: deployment pattern ladder from low cost to high confidence.

## Slide 7 - AI inference is strategic infrastructure

External APIs reduce time-to-market. Open-weight models reduce vendor dependency. Internal GPUs increase autonomy. Public/academic compute can support sovereignty.

Every option trades cost, latency, quality, security and dependence.

Visual: AI inference decision matrix.

## Slide 8 - The Bayesian gate

A decision should pass only if evidence supports: risk acceptable at 95%, margin acceptable at 95%, security exposure acceptable, sovereignty exposure acceptable, team blocking acceptable.

Visual: decision gate with posterior probability thresholds.

## Slide 9 - The core formula

Adjusted Margin = Price - Delivery Cost - Infrastructure Cost - Test Cost - Security Cost - AI Cost - Expected Residual Risk

The cheapest solution may fail once residual risk is priced.

Visual: formula with risk term highlighted.

## Slide 10 - Closing message

FinOps tells engineering what technology costs. Confidence-constrained engineering economics tells leadership what that cost buys.

The new trade-off is not cloud vs on-premise. It is confidence under constraints.

Visual: final statement on clean background.
